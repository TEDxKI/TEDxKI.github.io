TEDxKI 2026

Momentum

April 18 • Medicinska Föreningen Aula

Momentum: Ideas in Motion

The theme for this year “Momentum” describes the invisible force that turns sparks into revolutions, ideas into action, and individuals into movements.

Momentum is more than just the physics phenomenon of force or speed of an object in motion. It is the direction, purpose, and persistence that drives our actions to achieve a result. Whether it is a scientific breakthrough, a personal transformation, a cultural shift, or a societal challenge, momentum is what propels us forward.

At TEDxKI 2026, we celebrate the people and ideas that are in motion. This also strongly aligns with Karolinska Institutet’s motto of “striving for improved health”. We are building momentum to serve as the catalyst for healthcare and continuous progress in our community. Together, we are building momentum to spark new ideas, gaining momentum through shared knowledge, keeping momentum by sustaining innovation, and using momentum to create meaningful change.

We envision that this year’s talks can explore how momentum begins, how it grows, and how we can harness it to create lasting impact.

Speakers

Moa Ilar

National Cross-Country Skier
Two Olympics. Two World Championships. Three World Cup titles. One stage. At 28, Moa Ilar is one of Sweden’s leading cross-country skiers and a proud member of the national team. After claiming her first World Championship title in 2023 in the 20 km freestyle mass start, she went on to secure two more victories in Oslo (2025) and Oberhof (2026), and is now competing in her second Winter Olympics in 2026. On the TEDxKI stage, Moa Ilar will reflect on the obstacles she faced, the discipline it took to stay committed, and how learning to create her own momentum changed everything. Her message is simple yet powerful: find what you love — and dare to pursue it relentlessly. We are honored to welcome her to the TEDxKI stage this year.

Sam Hultin

Artist
Sam Hultin is a Stockholm-based artist whose work explores queer history, identity, and the connections between personal experience and political structures💭. Through city walks, sing-alongs, and collective commemorations, Sam invites communities to engage with shared histories and reflect on how they shape the present. Their work is represented in major Swedish collections, including Moderna Museet, Statens konstråd, Gothenburg Museum of Art and Malmö Art Museum🎨 🎤On the TEDxKI stage, Sam will share the story behind Eva-Lisa’s Monument, an artistic memory project about trans pioneer Eva-Lisa Bengtson and the legacy of queer organizing in Sweden. Their talk will explore how histories of gender and power shape today’s society and what solidarity truly means in practice 🏳️‍🌈🏳️‍⚧️ We are honored to welcome Sam Hultin to the TEDxKI stage this year 🌟

Johanna Nissén

Co-Founder & CEO
Swedish innovator Johanna Nissen, Co-Founder and CEO of Vividye, is transforming the future of cosmetic colouring through high-performance, sustainable innovation. A graduate of Chalmers University of Technology, she leads sales, partnerships and business development while driving collaborations with global industry leaders. Featured in BBC, Elle and Forbes, and part of the Prins Daniel Fellowship and Women Tech EU, she is passionate about turning breakthrough ideas into impactful commercial products. In her TEDx talk, she challenges us to rethink sustainability: it is not worth anything if it doesn’t make something better. Drawing parallels between her background as a competitive middle-distance runner and building a company, she shares why real innovation is a marathon — one that demands resilience, vision and the courage to improve what already exists. Excited and honoured to welcome her to the TEDxKI stage!

Sebastian Blomé

Researcher
Sebastian is pursuing his PhD in Medical Sciences at Karolinska Institutet. Like many of us, he also had a moment that made him realise he wanted to pursue medical research. On the TEDxKI stage, he will share with us a personal story how in the flash of a second his whole life changed and he was not who he used to be. This does not have to be a bad thing. Nature Advocate

Sara Hägg

Associate Professor
Sara Hägg leads a research group at Karolinska Institutet, working at the forefront of molecular epidemiology of aging. Internationally recognized for her work on biological aging, she explores how biomarkers reveal the way we age, and how they connect to age-related diseases 🔬✨ But this talk isn’t about her research. It’s about what it really means to be the person behind it navigating uncertainty, pressure, curiosity, and the constant pursuit of what is not yet understood 🧠💭 🎤On the TEDxKI stage, Sara takes us beyond publications and breakthroughs, into the lived reality of being a scientist today, and the moments when, against all odds, everything suddenly clicks 💫 We are honored to welcome her to the TEDxKI stage this year 🌟
